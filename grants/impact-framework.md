# Impact Framework — C.R.E.E.D. Institute

This document defines the metrics, targets, and measurement approach used to evaluate the impact of C.R.E.E.D. grant-funded work. Use this framework in grant applications (evaluation/impact sections) and in interim/final reports.

---

## Quantitative Metrics

| Metric | Baseline | 6-Month Target | 12-Month Target |
|--------|---------|----------------|----------------|
| **Governance events scored** | 500+ | 5,000 | 25,000 |
| **Organizations on platform** | 1 | 3 | 10 |
| **Active compliance grades issued** | 1 | 5 | 15 |
| **GitHub repository stars** | 0 | 50 | 200 |
| **External contributors (GitHub)** | 0 | 5 | 15 |
| **Peer-reviewed papers published** | 0 | 1 | 3 |
| **Conference presentations** | 0 | 2 | 4 |

**Baseline date**: April 2026 (grant start). Baselines are auditable from live platform data and public GitHub repository.

---

## Qualitative Outcomes

### Year 1

- **Platform maturity**: C.R.E.E.D. governance platform supports multi-organization deployments with organization-level dashboards, API access, and configurable rule sets.
- **Research credibility**: At least one peer-reviewed paper accepted at a recognized AI ethics or governance venue, establishing academic standing for the governance methodology.
- **Ecosystem integration**: Active working relationships with at least two Montreal academic institutions (UdeM, McGill, or Concordia) and one AI ecosystem organization (Mila, OBVIA, or Scale AI).
- **Public trust signal**: Live compliance badges deployed by at least 5 external organizations, demonstrating that governance verification is practical and adoptable beyond C.R.E.E.D.'s own platform.
- **Community building**: At least 100 unique visitors to creed-ai.org per month by month 12; at least 50 newsletter subscribers.

### Year 2–3

- **Governance standard influence**: C.R.E.E.D. rule sets referenced in at least one public policy consultation, regulatory submission, or standards body working group.
- **Research program depth**: All four research programs (Agency, Memory, Emotional Modeling, Governance) have published outputs and active researcher communities.
- **Self-sustaining community**: GitHub contributor community active without direct funding incentives; external organizations contributing rule sets and governance data.
- **Policy impact**: At least one governance recommendation from C.R.E.E.D. research adopted or cited in a public policy document (federal, provincial, or municipal level).
- **Financial sustainability**: Revenue-generating services (compliance consulting, hosted platform tier, training) covering at least 30% of operating costs without grant dependency.

## Measurement Approach

### Platform Telemetry
All governance events, scores, and grades are logged to the C.R.E.E.D. database in real time. Metrics (events scored, organizations, grades) are pulled directly from platform data. Monthly reports auto-generated and shared with funders on request.

**Verification**: Platform is publicly accessible at creed.kytranempowerment.com. Funders can inspect live data independently.

### GitHub Analytics
Repository stars, forks, contributors, and commit activity tracked via GitHub's native analytics. Exported monthly for reporting.

**Verification**: Public repository at github.com/KytranKatarn/kytran-creed. All data publicly auditable.

### Publications & Presentations
Peer-reviewed papers tracked via DOI and journal/conference acceptance letters. Presentation invitations and slide decks maintained in organizational records and shared with funders.

**Verification**: Accepted papers linked from creed-ai.org publications page. Open-access publication ensures free verifiability.

### Stakeholder Surveys
Annual survey to all organizations using the platform (5–10 minute questionnaire). Survey covers: perceived value, governance culture change, likelihood to recommend, feature requests.

Quarterly informal check-ins with academic partners. Notes logged in organizational CRM.

**Verification**: Survey results included in annual funder report. Anonymized aggregate data made publicly available.

### Policy Impact
Policy citations tracked via Google Scholar alerts, regulatory body newsletters, and direct outreach to policy researchers. Any citation or adoption logged in organizational impact register.

**Verification**: Policy impact register maintained in organizational records. Available to funders on request.

## Theory of Change

**If** C.R.E.E.D. builds open-source, real-time AI governance infrastructure **and** publishes rigorous research on AI ethics **and** builds an inclusive community of researchers, practitioners, and affected stakeholders **then** AI organizations will have a practical, credible path to verifiable governance, **which will** shift the AI ethics landscape from voluntary self-attestation toward accountable, continuous compliance — reducing harm and building public trust in AI systems.

The key assumption is that practical tooling (working software + clear metrics) lowers the barrier to adoption sufficiently that organizations will choose verifiable governance over performative compliance statements. Our early traction (A+ grade, 500+ events, open-source codebase) validates this assumption for our initial deployment context.
