# C.R.E.E.D. — Funder Profiles

Research on 5 target funders. Updated: 2026-04-14.

## 1. Coefficient Giving

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Range** | $50K–$5M |
| **Program** | "Navigating Transformative AI" |
| **Application Process** | Rolling basis — apply anytime |
| **Focus** | Governance infrastructure, policy tools, AI accountability frameworks |

**Why C.R.E.E.D. fits:**
Coefficient's "Navigating Transformative AI" stream is a direct match with C.R.E.E.D.'s mandate. They fund organizations building practical governance tools that scale, not just policy papers. C.R.E.E.D.'s open-source REST API scoring engine, live compliance badges, and A+-F grading system are exactly the kind of operational infrastructure this funder prioritizes.

**Alignment points:**
- Governance-by-design: C.R.E.E.D. embeds compliance at the event level, not retroactively
- Scalable infrastructure: REST API model means any AI system can integrate
- Measurable outcomes: compliance grade and event count are objective metrics
- Open-source commitment (AGPL-3.0) aligns with public-benefit orientation

**Next step:** Direct outreach to program officer with one-pager. Rolling basis means no deadline pressure — but early contact is valuable for relationship-building.

## 2. ISED Canada (Innovation, Science and Economic Development)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Range** | Up to $8.6M (AI standards pool) |
| **Program** | AI Standards and Responsible AI funding streams |
| **Application Process** | Government procurement / open calls — monitor Canada.ca/grants |
| **Focus** | Canadian-made AI standards, domestic AI governance infrastructure |

**Why C.R.E.E.D. fits:**
ISED has committed funding to develop Canadian AI standards capacity. As a Canadian nonprofit building open-source AI compliance tooling, C.R.E.E.D. is positioned to be a standards reference implementation — a platform where proposed Canadian AI governance rules can be encoded, tested, and scored at scale.

**Alignment points:**
- Canadian entity headquartered in Montreal, QC
- Open-source platform can be adopted by Canadian institutions and industry
- Rule engine architecture allows Canadian regulatory requirements to be encoded as rule packs
- Bridges academic research (4 programs) with operational tooling
- Potential to become reference implementation for ISED AI standards work

**Next step:** Monitor ISED open calls. Build relationships with Standards Council of Canada (SCC) as a gateway to ISED funding. Align future grant narrative with Canada's Pan-Canadian AI Strategy language.

## 3. Mozilla Foundation

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Range** | $50K–$250K |
| **Program** | Mozilla Technology Fund (MTF) |
| **Application Process** | Periodic open calls — watch mozillafoundation.org/en/what-we-fund/ |
| **Focus** | Open-source technology serving the public interest, AI accountability |

**Why C.R.E.E.D. fits:**
Mozilla Technology Fund explicitly supports open-source projects that hold AI systems accountable. C.R.E.E.D.'s AGPL-3.0 license, public governance dashboard, and community-auditable scoring engine align directly with Mozilla's values around internet health and AI transparency.

**Alignment points:**
- AGPL-3.0 license: strongest open-source copyleft, aligned with Mozilla's open-web values
- Public accountability: live dashboard at creed.kytranempowerment.com is publicly visible
- Not vendor-locked: any AI system, any organization can integrate via REST API
- Small team, high leverage: small grant would meaningfully advance the platform
- Mozilla has previously funded AI governance tooling at similar scale

**Next step:** Apply during next MTF open call cycle. Ensure project README and GitHub repo are polished before applying — Mozilla reviewers will examine the codebase directly.

## 4. CIFAR (Canadian Institute for Advanced Research)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Range** | Varies by program (typically $100K–$500K+ for AI & Society grants) |
| **Program** | AI & Society / Safe & Responsible AI |
| **Application Process** | By invitation or competitive open call — periodic; CIFAR HQ in Toronto, strong Montreal ecosystem ties |
| **Focus** | Interdisciplinary AI research, responsible AI, societal impact |

**Why C.R.E.E.D. fits:**
CIFAR anchors Canada's AI research ecosystem and has deep connections to Montreal's AI cluster (Mila, IVADO, UdeM, McGill, Concordia). C.R.E.E.D.'s 4 research programs — Artificial Agency, Memory Ethics, Emotional Modeling, and Governance Models — are genuine interdisciplinary research questions that sit squarely within CIFAR's AI & Society mandate.

**Alignment points:**
- Montreal location connects to Mila and CIFAR's existing AI researcher networks
- Research programs are academically substantive, not just product documentation
- CIFAR values translation from research to practice — C.R.E.E.D. embodies this with a live platform
- V.I.G.I.L. (AI Ethics Director) demonstrates novel human-AI governance collaboration worth studying
- Potential for joint publications with CIFAR-affiliated researchers

**Next step:** Build relationships with Mila-affiliated researchers first. A co-application with a Mila or UdeM researcher significantly increases odds. CIFAR often funds by invitation after relationship development. Target 2026–2027 application cycle.

## 5. SSHRC (Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Range** | $200K–$2.5M over 4–7 years |
| **Program** | Partnership Grants |
| **Application Process** | Annual competitions — requires academic co-PI; apply once partnership is formed |
| **Focus** | Academic-community partnerships, long-term research with societal benefit |

**Why C.R.E.E.D. fits:**
SSHRC Partnership Grants are designed for exactly the kind of sustained, interdisciplinary research C.R.E.E.D.'s 4 programs represent. The scale ($200K–$2.5M) and duration (4–7 years) would allow genuine research depth. The ethical questions around AI memory, emotional modeling, and governance are squarely in humanities and social science territory.

**Alignment points:**
- Research programs align with SSHRC priority areas: ethics, governance, social impact of technology
- Montreal location provides access to UdeM, McGill, and Concordia as academic co-PI candidates
- C.R.E.E.D. as a nonprofit community partner fits the Partnership Grant structure
- Long-term funding would allow longitudinal governance data collection at scale
- AGPL-3.0 open-source outputs satisfy SSHRC open access requirements

**Requirement — Academic Co-PI:** SSHRC Partnership Grants require a Canadian academic institution as lead applicant with a faculty co-PI. C.R.E.E.D. would be the community/nonprofit partner.

**Next step:** This is a 2027+ target. First priority is forming a formal research partnership with a faculty member at UdeM, McGill, or Concordia. Attend Mila events and IVADO workshops to build these relationships organically.
