## The Problem

AI governance today is failing in four critical ways:

- **Retrospective**: Audits happen after harm, not during operation. By the time a governance failure is detected, real damage has been done to people, communities, and trust.
- **Voluntary**: Compliance frameworks (EU AI Act, Montreal Declaration, OECD Principles) rely on self-attestation. There is no independent, continuous verification mechanism.
- **Opaque**: Most AI systems — including autonomous agents — operate as black boxes. Stakeholders have no visibility into decision-making, value alignment, or behavioral drift over time.
- **Siloed**: Governance standards, enforcement bodies, and academic researchers operate in separate worlds. There is no shared, open infrastructure for comparing compliance across organizations or platforms.

The result is an AI ecosystem where "ethical AI" is a marketing claim, not a measurable property. As autonomous agents take on higher-stakes roles — healthcare, legal, financial, public services — the absence of real-time governance infrastructure poses a systemic risk.

---

## Our Solution

C.R.E.E.D. has built an open-source, event-driven governance platform that makes AI compliance continuous, verifiable, and public:

- **Real-time monitoring**: Every AI agent action is captured as a structured governance event and evaluated against a rule set derived from established ethical frameworks.
- **Automated scoring**: Events are scored across dimensions including transparency, fairness, autonomy preservation, and harm avoidance. Scores aggregate into an overall compliance grade (A+ through F).
- **Verifiable badges**: Organizations display live compliance badges tied to actual platform data — not self-reported scores.
- **LCARS dashboard**: A public governance dashboard at creed.kytranempowerment.com shows live event feeds, scores, and trends. All data is inspectable.

**Current state**: The platform is live and operational. We are scoring 500+ governance events generated by the A.R.C.H.I.E. autonomous agent platform (16 AI agents, 10+ departments). Current compliance grade: **A+**. All 16 rule tests are passing. The full codebase is open source at github.com/KytranKatarn/kytran-creed.

This is proof-of-concept at organizational scale. The requested funding will allow us to extend the platform to external organizations, deepen the research programs, and build the Montreal ecosystem partnerships needed for lasting impact.

## Research Programs

C.R.E.E.D. runs four interconnected research programs that feed directly into platform development:

**Program A — Artificial Agency & Moral Risk**
Investigates the conditions under which autonomous AI agents can be held morally responsible for outcomes, and what governance structures are necessary when they cannot. Outputs: policy briefs, rule-set contributions to the platform, academic papers.

**Program B — Memory, Continuity & Deletion Ethics**
Explores the ethical implications of AI systems that persist context, form habits, and accumulate knowledge about individuals over time. Addresses right-to-erasure, continuity of identity, and the ethics of AI "forgetting." Outputs: governance rules for memory-enabled agents, researcher toolkits, policy recommendations.

**Program C — Emotional Modeling & Non-Exploitation**
Examines the ethics of AI systems that simulate emotional states or build emotional rapport with users. Develops rules for detecting and preventing exploitation of emotional vulnerability. Outputs: detection heuristics, compliance rules, stakeholder guidance documents.

**Program D — Governance & Representation Models**
Develops frameworks for inclusive AI governance — ensuring that affected communities, not just developers and deployers, have meaningful input into how AI systems are governed. Outputs: participatory governance models, open consultation tools, policy templates.
