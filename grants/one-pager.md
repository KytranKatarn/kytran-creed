## The Problem

AI systems are deployed faster than governance frameworks can keep pace. Organizations lack standardized compliance tooling, ethics reviews happen reactively — after harm has already occurred — and there is no accessible, open infrastructure for continuous AI accountability. The result: unchecked systems, eroded public trust, and growing regulatory risk.

---

## Our Solution

C.R.E.E.D. is an open-source AI governance platform that makes compliance continuous and measurable.

- **Events via REST API** — any AI system can emit governance events in real time
- **Scoring engine** — every event is evaluated against configurable ethical rule sets
- **Compliance grades A+–F** — transparent, human-readable accountability scores
- **SVG badges** — embed live compliance status in any dashboard or README
- **Live dashboard** — governance activity, trends, and incident history at a glance

## Research Programs

1. **Artificial Agency** — When do AI systems bear ethical responsibility? Developing frameworks for attribution and accountability in autonomous decision-making.
2. **Memory Ethics** — Privacy, consent, and the right to be forgotten in AI systems that retain persistent memory.
3. **Emotional Modeling** — Risks and responsibilities of AI systems that simulate emotional states or detect user affect.
4. **Governance Models** — Comparative research on regulatory approaches across jurisdictions, feeding directly into C.R.E.E.D.'s rule engine.
